Know Before You Click
Learn the 6 most common phishing red flags before testing your skills.
🔗
Suspicious URLs
Phishers mimic real domains with subtle typos or extra subdomains. Always hover to preview links before clicking.
secure-paypal.com.verify-account.net
📮
Mismatched Sender
The display name may say "Apple Support" but the actual email address is from a random domain.
Apple Support <noreply@apple-account-update.xyz>
⏰
Urgency & Fear Tactics
Phrases like "Your account will be suspended in 24 hours!" are designed to bypass your rational thinking and make you act fast.
"ACT NOW — verify immediately or lose access!"
🎁
Too-Good-To-Be-True Offers
Winning a lottery you never entered, or a surprise refund — if it sounds too good to be true, it almost certainly is.
"You've been selected for a $1,000 Amazon gift card!"
📎
Unexpected Attachments
Malware is often hidden in .zip, .exe, or even Office files. Legitimate services rarely send unsolicited attachments.
invoice_unpaid_FINAL.zip · document_scan.exe
✍️
Poor Grammar & Formatting
Mass phishing emails often contain spelling mistakes, awkward phrasing, or inconsistent formatting that legitimate companies avoid.
"Dear Costumer, you're account need verify."
